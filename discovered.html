About the App

March 2017 - April 2017

Discovered is a mobile applicationan that aims to help with the Syrian refugee crisis by making the transition into Canadian culture an enjoyable, social experience. This app brings public city events into one consolidated and easy-to-read mobile application so that users can check in to see what classes, events, and activities are being put on by city services like libraries, rec centres, parks, and more so that those who are new to living in Canada have ample opportunity to get out and connect with their communities.

UI UX Wireframing

Skills Gained

  • Working with a team to come up with an appropriate solution to a current social problem.
  • Efficiency working with wireframes to create a final frontend working prototype.
  • Working with weekly critiques to refine and finalize our application.

Role

  • Researching about the current Syrian crisis and common problems they are facing
  • Designing the project UI and flow through the application.
  • Working on interaction and feedback through the application to create a clearly cohesive flow.

Process

This project was completed in a group of 4 over the course of a month. We first began by researching about common issues refugees arriving into Canada have, and what possible solutions we can provide.

We looked specifically at the issue of integration problems many people of a new community face within Canada. Our approach to this was to create a social mobile application that displays the user with community events based on their location. It also highlights special events and programs for immigrants such as English learning programs and immigration help services.

Initially, we set our timeline to begin initial wireframing, and then from there create working prototypes to user test. After testing our application, we then refined our application based on our findings and worked towards our final output.
